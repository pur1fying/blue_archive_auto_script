Guide for activity sweep fill in (remaining-count budget model):

"Activity sweep stage" and "Activity sweep remaining count" are both comma-separated and paired in order.

The remaining count records how many sweeps are still needed for each stage and persists across runs: each successful sweep decrements the actual swept count and writes it back; when it reaches 0 the stage is skipped.

Use -1 to mean the stage is swept last with unlimited times (sweep as much as current AP allows); -1 is never decremented.

Actual sweeps per stage this run = min(remaining count, current AP / per-sweep AP cost); for -1 stages = current AP / per-sweep AP cost.

Sweep Multiple Stages:

Stages and remaining counts are comma-separated and paired; all finite-count stages are swept first (in written order), then -1 stages.

Example:

Sweep stage: 9, 10, 11

Remaining count: 25, 3, -1

Means: sweep stage 9 (up to 25 times, decremented and saved), stage 10 (up to 3 times), then stage 11 last (unlimited, as much AP allows).

On the next run, stages 9 and 10 continue from their remaining counts; stage 11 stays -1.
